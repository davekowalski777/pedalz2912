The Standard Pedal Order

While there's room for experimentation, here's the traditional order that works for most situations:

1

Tuner

Place your tuner first to get the cleanest signal for accurate tuning. It should also have a bypass feature to mute your signal when tuning.

2

Dynamics/Filters

  • Wah pedal
  • Compressor
  • EQ
  • Volume pedal (can also go last)
3

Gain Stages

  • Overdrive
  • Distortion
  • Fuzz
Arrange from lowest to highest gain for the most versatile sound options.
4

Modulation

  • Chorus
  • Flanger
  • Phaser
  • Tremolo
5

Time-Based Effects

  • Delay
  • Reverb
Reverb usually goes last to create a natural space for all other effects.

Signal Chain Considerations

Technical Factors

  • Buffer placement
    Start and end of chain for signal integrity
  • Cable length and quality
    Use high-quality, shorter cables when possible
  • Power supply isolation
    Prevents unwanted noise and interference
  • True bypass vs. buffered bypass
    Balance based on your total chain length

Common Variations

  • Volume pedal can go before or after drive pedals
  • Wah can go after drive for a different character
  • Some players prefer modulation before drive
  • Parallel effects loops for more complex setups

Pro Tips

  • Experiment with different orders to find your perfect tone
  • Consider using a loop switcher for complex setups
  • Keep your most-used effects easily accessible
